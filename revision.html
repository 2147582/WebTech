The World Wide Web project was first proposed in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee during his time as a software engineer in CERN (other known as the European Organization for Nuclear Research). His proposal document was initially titled “Information Management: A Proposal”, but then it was not granted by his superior for where CERN is not focused on network project. The document contains the main fundamentals of the web that the we are still using, which is the HTML, HTTP, and URI.
The first web page was created and hosted on the NeXT computer in the year 1990, and it was the time when the first web page was published. By the year 1993, CERN announced that world wide web can be used by anyone. On the year 1994 went to Massachusetts Institute of Technology and found World Wide Web Consortium, also known as W3C.
